# mem21.md: Distance as the Primary Travel Metric

For a relativistic traveler, the primary measurable change is not an increase in local speed, but a contraction of the spatial distance to the destination. As energy is expended to change reference frames, the proper distance to a target decreases according to the Lorentz factor. Therefore, interstellar travel is structurally better understood as the manipulation of spatial geometry (shrinking the universe) rather than the accumulation of kinetic velocity.
