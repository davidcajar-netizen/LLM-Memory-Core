# mem20.md: The Zero-Velocity Truth

In Special Relativity, an observer is always at rest (0 velocity) within their own reference frame. Stating that an object is moving at a specific percentage of the speed of light is only meaningful when compared to a separate, external reference frame. Locally, velocity is a null concept; one does not "feel" speed, only acceleration. The perception of motion is entirely dependent on the relational geometry between two distinct frames of reference.
