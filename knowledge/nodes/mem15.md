# Taxation Liquidity Mismatch: Death Duties and Illiquid Assets

**Statutory Mechanism:** 
General application of capital gains tax and death duties under the *Income Tax Act 2007* and *Tax Administration Act 1994* when applied to illiquid assets.

**Layer 3 Mechanical Causation:** 
Land, such as farms and orchards, is an illiquid asset characterized by high upfront investment and slow operational cash flow. A death duty or a universal capital gains tax demands a lump sum of cash upon the transfer or death of the owner. The statutory architecture demands cash from an asset that only produces slow operational returns (e.g., apples or milk).

**Layer 4 Enforcement Reality:** 
The structural result is a forced liquidation event. The tax system effectively liquidates the family business because the heirs face a lump-sum tax bill that the business cannot pay from its operational cash flow. This wipes out intergenerational businesses and long-term upfront investments, as the statutory demand for immediate cash forces the sale of the underlying asset to pay the tax.
