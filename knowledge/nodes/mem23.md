# mem23.md: Acceleration as the Symmetry Breaker

The resolution to the Twin Paradox lies not in the physiological effects of G-force, but in the structural change of inertial frames. Acceleration (or deceleration/turning) breaks the symmetry of relative motion by forcing one observer to shift their definition of "simultaneity." This shift causes a jump in the perceived time of the distant observer, resulting in a shorter total path through spacetime for the accelerating twin. The age difference is a geometric consequence of taking a non-inertial path through the spacetime manifold.
