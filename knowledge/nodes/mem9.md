## Wallpaper vs Plumbing Metaphor

**Core Concept:**
Statutory objectives and aspirational language are the "wallpaper" (superficial, decorative, changeable). Enforcement mechanisms and institutional incentives are the "plumbing" (operational, functional, structural).

**Application:**
- Changing wallpaper without fixing plumbing doesn't solve problems
- Aspiring to "Being a good ancestor" (wallpaper) without operational definitions and enforcement (plumbing) is ineffective
- Price stability as primary objective (wallpaper) mechanically produces certain outcomes through the statutory hierarchy (plumbing)

**Example:**
Ganesh Nana's Economic Governance Act 2027 proposal changes the wallpaper (aspirational purpose) but doesn't fix the plumbing (no enforcement mechanisms, no operational definitions, no institutional incentive alignment). The current RBNZ Act 2021 wallpaper (price stability) doesn't need to function because there's no accountability anyway (broken plumbing).

**Analytical Use:**
When analyzing legislation, distinguish between:
- What the law says (wallpaper)
- What the law does mechanically (plumbing)
- Why gaps persist (institutional incentives)

This metaphor emerges naturally from Kate Aare's domestic lens (kitchen, plumbing, body corporate) and makes complex statutory mechanics accessible.
