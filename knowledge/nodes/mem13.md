# Taxation Enforcement Gap: Commissioner's Arbitrary Valuation Power

**Statutory Mechanism:** 
*Tax Administration Act 1994*, Section 113 (Commissioner's power to amend assessments) and Section 357 (Commissioner's power to determine the value of a thing).

**Layer 3 Mechanical Causation:** 
The Inland Revenue Department relies on self-assessment and retrospective audit. For illiquid, unique assets like specific blocks of farmland or orchards, value is highly subjective and depends on soil quality, water rights, and localized market conditions. The Commissioner possesses broad statutory authority to amend assessments and determine value without needing to prove the taxpayer's declared value was fraudulent, only that their own assessment is necessary to ensure correctness.

**Layer 4 Enforcement Reality:** 
The enforcement machinery is structurally weaponized against the transparent middle class. The ordinary farmer faces massive compliance costs and arbitrary valuations, bearing the burden of proof to show the Commissioner's valuation is excessive in a resource-intensive disputes process. Meanwhile, the opaque elite hold their assets inside complex trust structures embedded in the *Income Tax Act 2007*. The enforcement apparatus lacks the operational capacity to audit these opaque structures, meaning a universal capital gains tax punishes the transparent middle class while the opaque wealthy slip through the cracks.
