# Health Procurement: Pharmac's Monopsony and Statutory Rationing

**Statutory Mechanism:** 
*Pae Ora (Healthy Futures) Act 2022*, Section 68 (objective to secure best health outcomes within funding provided), Section 69 (pharmaceutical schedule management), and Section 74 (exemption from Part 2 of the *Commerce Act 1986*). Governed as an Autonomous Crown Entity under the *Crown Entities Act 2004*.

**Layer 3 Mechanical Causation:** 
Pharmac operates as a legal monopsony (a single buyer) and is explicitly exempt from the anti-competitive behavior laws that govern normal businesses. Its statutory mandate is to negotiate the best price for the New Zealand market within a strictly fixed domestic budget. The negotiation is not enforced by a fair referee, but by three statutory levers: a hard budget cap, the threat of total market exclusion (refusing to list the drug on the schedule), and the statutory exemption from the *Commerce Act 1986*.

**Layer 4 Enforcement Reality:** 
The operational reality of having "our own people negotiate" is statutory rationing. Pharmac is legally mandated to say no to life-saving treatments if the price exceeds their fixed budget. If New Zealand were to piggyback on Australian negotiated prices, it would immediately blow the fixed domestic budget. The statute forces a zero-sum reality: blowing the budget today to save a few lives now kills future patients by draining the fund for tomorrow's treatments. The system successfully enforces a brutal rationing of a finite budget to protect the domestic fund at the expense of vulnerable patients denied unfunded drugs.
