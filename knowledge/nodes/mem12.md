## Critique of Economic Governance Act 2027 Proposal

Layer 1 and Layer 2 analysis of Ganesh Nana's proposed Economic Governance Act 2027.

**Layer 1 (Symptom):** Identifies siloed policy-making, short-term horizons, and growth obsession. Lacks empirical evidence for harm.

**Layer 2 (Attribution):** Blames the constitutional and legislative framework (PFA 1989 s 26G, RBNZ Act 2021 s 9, Regulatory Standards Act 2025). Asserts causation without tracing statutory mechanics.

**Layer 3 (Architecture):** Proposes replacing statutory hierarchy with aspirational language ("Being a good ancestor").

**Mechanical failure:** "Being a good ancestor" is not a justiciable standard. The proposal uses circular definitions ("productive resources" = resources "able to deliver outcomes in line with the purpose").

**Layer 4 (Enforcement):** Creates an Economic Governance Council with no decision-making power or penalty for non-compliance.

**Verdict:** The proposal changes the wallpaper without fixing the plumbing. It lacks operational definitions, enforcement mechanisms, and institutional incentive alignment.
