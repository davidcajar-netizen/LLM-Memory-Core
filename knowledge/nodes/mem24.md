# mem24.md: The Light Speed Energy Asymptote

Light speed ($c$) is not a barrier that can be approached locally, as light always recedes from any massive observer at exactly $c$. The "limit" is an energy asymptote where the energy required to further alter one's relationship to the rest of the universe approaches infinity. Relativistic effects (time dilation and length contraction) are the operational manifestations of this increasing energy cost, not the result of "catching up" to a photon.
