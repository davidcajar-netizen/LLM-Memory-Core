# mem22.md: The Symmetry of Relativistic Mass-Energy

Kinetic energy and relativistic mass are not intrinsic properties of an object but are relational states between reference frames. If Observer A sees Observer B moving at 0.99c, Observer B equally sees Observer A moving at 0.99c. Both observers perceive the other as having increased energy/mass. There is no absolute "holder" of relativistic energy during periods of constant velocity; the energy state is entirely symmetric until a frame change occurs.
