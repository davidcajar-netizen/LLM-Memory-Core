## Insight
A coherent logical critique of campaign finance transparency regimes posits that by mandating disclosure of donations (the potential instrument of undue influence), the system does not prevent the corrupting act but rather regulates and records it. This effectively legitimizes the activity of seeking leverage, which was the original corrupting element the laws aimed to combat.

## Details
-   The argument draws an analogy: making murder legal provided the murderer is publicly identified does not solve murder; it legalizes it.
-   The critique highlights a perceived fundamental contradiction: a system designed to combat corruption by making its mechanisms transparent may instead institutionalize those mechanisms.
-   This frames transparency not as a solution to corruption, but as societal surrender to its inevitability, shifting the goal from prevention to observation.
